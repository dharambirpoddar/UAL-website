Thesis :- Noise and Flow Field due to Interaction of compressible Vortex Ring with Generic Shape bodies: PIV study

The shock and wall interaction of vortex ring for shock Mach number, M varying from 1.35 to 1.76 is to be studied. The aim is to find the cause of noise production and the level of noise associated with the flow and its directivity. The vortex ring wall interaction is characterized using the translational velocity and diameter variation of the vortex ring in axial and radial directions. The ring slows down in the presence of wall and its diameter increases (called stretching). Discrete events associated with vortex ring-wall interaction are impingement, deformation, stretching, secondary wall vortex growth and lift-off. These events are to be identified by performing wavelet transform on the fluctuating pressure signal and should be verified with flow visualization pictures of TR-PIV. The aim is also to measure the wall pressure during the head on collision of shock and vortex ring interaction with the wall to calculate the impulse and the decay parameter. Finally how the reflected shock interacts with the vortex ring is also to be studied. Both the unsteady velocity fields and vorticity fields are measured using Time Resolved PIV because the noise generated is also due to the unsteady changes of vorticity. Schlieren study of shock and vortex ring wall interaction is also aimed in the future.
